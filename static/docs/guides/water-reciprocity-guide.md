## How Reciprocity Works

### The Reciprocity Score

The UBEC system continuously monitors your economic relationships and calculates a **Reciprocity Score** based on:

| Factor | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Balance Ratio** | How much you give vs. how much you receive |
| **Unique Partners** | Number of different people you exchange with |
| **Flow Consistency** | Regular exchanges vs. one-time transactions |
| **Mutual Benefit** | Whether both parties gain from exchanges |

### The Ideal: Balanced Flow

Perfect reciprocity isn't about keeping exact score—it's about maintaining healthy flows where everyone participates in both giving and receiving.

**Healthy Reciprocity Looks Like:**
- You share your skills with others
- Others share their skills with you
- Value circulates through the community
- No one is always giving or always receiving

**Unhealthy Patterns:**
- Always giving, never receiving (martyr pattern)
- Always receiving, never giving (extraction pattern)
- Only exchanging with one person (isolation)
- Large, infrequent transactions instead of regular flow

## Earning UBECrc Tokens

UBECrc tokens are earned through reciprocal participation. The system evaluates:

### 1. Transaction Flow Analysis

The system tracks the flow of value in your transactions:

```
Reciprocity Ratio = Total Sent ÷ Total Received
```

- **Ratio ≈ 1.0** = Perfect balance (ideal)
- **Ratio > 1.0** = Net giver (also valuable)
- **Ratio < 1.0** = Net receiver (may need rebalancing)

### 2. Relationship Diversity

Exchanging with many different partners strengthens the network:

- 1-5 partners = Building foundation
- 5-15 partners = Active participant
- 15+ partners = Network connector

### 3. Flow Metrics

The system measures circulation velocity—how actively value moves:

- Regular small exchanges = Healthy circulation
- Hoarding or stagnation = Poor flow
- Active participation = Strong flow health

## Understanding Your Flow

### Flow Transaction Records

Every exchange in the UBECrc system is recorded as a **Flow Transaction** with:

- **From Account** — Who sent value
- **To Account** — Who received value
- **Amount** — How much was exchanged
- **Direction** — Inbound, Outbound, or Circular
- **Memo** — Description of the exchange

### Your Reciprocity Balance

Your **Reciprocity Balance** shows:

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Total Received** | Sum of all value received |
| **Total Sent** | Sum of all value given |
| **Net Flow** | Difference (positive = net receiver) |
| **Reciprocity Ratio** | Sent ÷ Received (1.0 = balanced) |
| **Transaction Count** | Total exchanges |
| **Unique Partners** | Number of different people |

## Best Practices for Healthy Flow

### ✅ DO

- Engage in regular, consistent exchanges
- Build relationships with diverse partners
- Give generously when you have abundance
- Ask for help when you need it (receiving is also giving!)
- Document exchanges with clear memos
- Celebrate reciprocal successes

### ❌ DON'T

- Hoard resources or avoid exchanges
- Only give without ever receiving
- Only receive without ever giving
- Limit exchanges to just one or two partners
- Focus on exact score-keeping
- Extract value without contributing

## Troubleshooting

**"My reciprocity score is low"**
- Review your give/receive ratio
- Consider whether you're predominantly giving or receiving
- Seek opportunities for the side you're lacking
- Build relationships with new partners

**"I don't know what to exchange"**
- Everyone has something to offer—skills, time, knowledge, resources
- Ask community members what they need
- Offer what you have in abundance
- Start small and build from there

**"Exchanges feel awkward"**
- Remember: reciprocity is natural and healthy
- It's not about keeping score—it's about relationship
- Both giving and receiving strengthen community
- Practice makes it easier
